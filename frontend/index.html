The art of computing.

“The beauty of math is that it is pure.”

Garbage in, garbage out, what a beautiful symphony. Many people blame computers for things that go wrong, but the beauty of computers is that they are as neutral as neutral can be.

This is an article by Adrian Anin

Connect a wire wrongly during setup? Yeah, I won’t come on. Forget to backup or save? Yeah, that’s your problem, not mine. Miss a semi-colon ? Yeah, I won’t run either. How frustrating yet humbling — a rookie mistake nonetheless. A computer program is like a mirror, you get basically what you give it. It’s like a parrot that simply repeats what it hears and reveals the heart and mind of the programmer.

It’s the logic errors that hurt the most. Those moments when you may feel like you’re just not enough or smart enough, don’t worry; everybody has them. Therein lies the power of community.

Every programmer can agree with the power of community. Your humble, lap-sized neutral friend is just revealing to you to the plain truth: that you don’t know it all. You couldn’t possibly remember it all, could you ? You couldn’t possibly have a panoramic view of the whole world of computing from the 19th century until now. In an age where information doubles nearly every 12 hours, this is even more profound. So take that pick-me-up and ask for help; ask that colleague who seems to understand it better; ask in that group, and don’t feel stupid for doing so. Someone can see outside the black box you're in; someone is waiting at the valley's apex with smiles and good news from the other side:

community is the key!

Don’t become a help vampire though; everyone must learn to face their own challenges. Enjoy challenges; they are your bread. They nourish you and make you better at what you do. The brain loves challenges and grows by solving them, so don’t starve your brain. Always remember, like Obi-Wan Kenobi, you have the higher ground (cue the Star Wars Jedi theme).

You can listen to the theme on YouTube

Art is truly universal, and viewing computing as an art, the same can be said for it. Everyone is an innate techie; it’s all about perception and consciousness. It's similar to how you would approach anything new: you learn how to use it, make some mistakes, and then ask someone who knows how to do it better than you to assist you (see... community).

The same applies to tech; all you need to do is avoid the gate keepers and you’ll be fine. Technology is advancing at an outstanding rate, and we must all harness our innate technological skills to avoid being left behind.

Your future tech-inclined self will thank you for taking this bold step to become a bird with wings spread in the fiercely blowing wind of technological advancement. So let go of your non-technical tether and soar on the beautiful winds of computing.
